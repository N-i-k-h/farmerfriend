Arecanut Diseases

Yele Chukki Roga

Symptoms

  • Yellowing of Leaves: The leaves turn yellow as an early sign.
  • Chlorosis: Chlorotic patches or streaks weaken the plant.
  • Drying of Leaf Tips: The leaf tips turn dry and brown.
  • Defoliation: Leaves fall off prematurely as the disease progresses.
  • Reduced Yield: Overall yield decreases due to damaged leaves.

Causes

The disease is caused by fungal infections triggered by poor soil conditions, excessive moisture, and improper agricultural practices. It may also spread through infected tools or machinery.

Management

  1. Pruning: Remove infected leaves and stems to control the spread.
  2. Fungicide Application: Use copper-based fungicides to manage the fungal growth.
  3. Soil Management: Ensure proper soil drainage and avoid waterlogging.
  4. Resistant Varieties: Use disease-resistant Arecanut varieties.
  5. Good Agricultural Practices: Follow proper spacing, irrigation, and farm sanitation.

Haladi Roga

Symptoms

  • Yellowing of Leaves: The disease starts with yellowing of the leaves.
  • Chlorotic Spots: Yellow or light brown spots appear on the leaves.
  • Leaf Streaks: Yellow or brown streaks develop along the veins.
  • Premature Leaf Drop: The affected leaves fall off prematurely.
  • Reduced Nut Size: The Arecanut produced is smaller and of inferior quality.

Causes

Haladi Roga is caused by fungal infections, primarily from the *Fusarium* species. Excessive moisture, poor drainage, and improper irrigation practices contribute to its spread.

Management

  1. Pruning: Regularly remove infected leaves and stems to prevent the spread.
  2. Fungicide Treatment: Apply fungicides like Mancozeb or Carbendazim to manage fungal growth.
  3. Soil and Water Management: Ensure good drainage and avoid waterlogging.
  4. Avoid Overcrowding: Provide adequate spacing between Arecanut trees for better air circulation.
  5. Use Disease-resistant Varieties: Opt for Arecanut varieties resistant to fungal infections.

Preventive Measures

  • Sanitation: Regularly clean tools and equipment to prevent fungal spores from spreading.
  • Regular Monitoring: Inspect plants frequently and treat symptoms early.
  • Proper Fertilization: Use balanced fertilizers to strengthen plant immunity.

Kole Roga

Symptoms

  • Yellowing of Leaves: The initial symptom is yellowing of older leaves from the tips.
  • Water-soaked Spots: Brown, water-soaked spots appear on leaves, turning yellow and dry.
  • Leaf Curling: Leaves may curl and become deformed or twisted.
  • Leaf Scorching: Edges of leaves scorch, especially in the lower parts.
  • Reduced Yield: Stunted growth, reduced flowering, and lower nut yield.

Causes

Kole Roga is caused by bacterial infections, particularly *Xanthomonas* species. Excessive moisture, poor drainage, and improper irrigation spread the disease.

Management

  1. Pruning: Remove and destroy infected plant parts to reduce bacterial spread.
  2. Antibacterial Treatment: Use copper-based bactericides like Bordeaux mixture.
  3. Soil and Water Management: Improve drainage and avoid stagnant water.
  4. Avoid Overcrowding: Maintain adequate spacing for air circulation.
  5. Use Disease-free Planting Material: Always use healthy seedlings or planting material.

Preventive Measures

  • Sanitation: Clean tools and irrigation systems regularly to prevent bacteria spread.
  • Regular Monitoring: Inspect plants frequently for early symptoms and treat them promptly.
  • Balanced Fertilization: Provide balanced fertilizers to enhance plant immunity.

Beru Hula Roga

Symptoms

  • Small Holes on Leaves: Small, round holes appear on the surface of the leaves.
  • Slight Wilting: Leaves may start to wilt and turn yellow.
  • Deformed Leaves: Affected leaves may become deformed, showing signs of necrosis or browning.
  • Presence of Insect Frass: Blackish powdery insect droppings (frass) appear on the affected areas.
  • Reduced Growth and Yield: The plant's growth is stunted, and the yield quality declines.

Causes

Beru Hula Roga is caused by the attack of the Arecanut weevil (*Rhynchophorus* species). The adult weevils lay eggs on the plant, and the larvae burrow into the plant, damaging it.

Management

  1. Mechanical Control: Remove and destroy affected leaves or plant parts.
  2. Insecticide Application: Apply systemic insecticides like Imidacloprid or Chlorpyrifos during the early infestation stages.
  3. Trapping: Use pheromone or light traps to reduce adult weevil populations.
